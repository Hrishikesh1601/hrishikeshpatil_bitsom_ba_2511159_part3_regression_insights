Residual Analysis


After completing the multiple regression model, I compared the actual monthly sales with the sales predicted by the model. The difference between these two values is called the residual.

Residual = Actual Sales − Predicted Sales

If the residual is close to zero, it means the prediction was quite accurate. A larger positive or negative residual means there was a bigger difference between the actual and predicted sales.


My Observations

After checking the residual values, I noticed that the model predicted monthly sales reasonably well for most of the stores. While a few stores had larger differences between actual and predicted sales, most of the residuals were fairly small.

This tells me that the regression model is able to explain a large part of the sales pattern. At the same time, it also shows that there are other business factors affecting sales that were not included in this analysis.



Conclusion

Overall, the residual analysis helped me understand how well the final regression model performed. The predictions were close to the actual sales for most stores, which gives confidence that the model is useful for analysing monthly sales.

However, no model can capture every real-world situation. Factors like customer behaviour, local events, competition, weather, and management decisions can also influence sales. Because of this, the regression results should be used as a guide for decision-making rather than the only basis for business decisions.
